Summary

I will implement a parallel version of the CKY algorithm used for parsing natural language.

Background

Parsing is a fundamental component of many applications in natural language processing, including machine translation, semantic analysis, and information extraction. Since natural language is so vast and ambiguous, attempts at constructing a formal grammar result in hundreds of thousands of nonterminal rules and tens of thousands of terminal rules. The CKY algorithm is a dynamic programming algorithm that parses in a worst case complexity of $O(|G|n^3)$, where $|G|$ denotes the size of the grammar and $n$ denotes the length of the sentence.
